Wakati Wetu! It's our time for Reparations Festival
Dates: 22–24 October 2025
Location: Entim Sidai, Nairobi


Wakati Wetu: It’s Our Time for Reparations is a three-day cultural and advocacy festival rooted in creativity, justice, and community. Timed with the African Union’s declaration of 2025 as the Year of Justice and Reparations for Africans and People of African Descent, the festival aims to spark a continental and diasporic conversation on reparative justice.Hosted in Nairobi—a regional hub of activism, culture, and policy influence—the event will convene over 400 participants including artists, youth organizers, educators, journalists, and movement leaders. Through music, theatre, visual arts, film, panels, storytelling, and shared meals, participants will engage with themes of racial injustice, climate and economic repair, cultural restitution, and gendered harm.
Organized in partnership with Baraza Media Lab, Deep South Solidarity Fund, Reform Initiatives, and African Union ECOSOCC, the festival will blend reflection, celebration, and mobilization—creating a vibrant, accessible space for truth-telling, healing, and imagination.
Expected Outcomes • Increased public understanding of reparations for colonial crimes and contemporary racial injustices in Africa and Europe
• Strengthened cross-border and intersectoral networks
• Renewed energy and strategy for long-term collaboration across Africa and the Global Africa
