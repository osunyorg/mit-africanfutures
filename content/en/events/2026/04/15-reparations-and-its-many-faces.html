African Futures Lab joinED  Observatório da Branquitude , The Guardian and Libera to address the many faces of reparation and the urgency of this global agenda. This event at the United Nations Permanent Forum on People of African Descent in Geneva conveneD to dissect how the legacy of slavery and colonialism produces the structural racism, poverty, and violence that Black populations endure across all continents. The session interrogateD the systems of privilege that obstruct global progress to ensure that reparative justice becomes a reality for all. Our Executive Director Liliane Umubyeyi, PhD and Parnerships & Advocacy Officer Hélène Himmer were in the panel.
